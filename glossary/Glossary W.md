### **w‑Coordinate (Inverse Radius)**

**Definition:**
 The **w‑coordinate** is defined as
$$
w=\frac{1}{r}
$$


where r is the radial distance from the origin. This transformation maps the infinite radial domain \(r\in [0,\infty )\)

 into the finite interval \(w\in (0,1]\).

**Context within RR and GR**

The w‑coordinate is used in the **mass domain** of the RR framework, where the geometry corresponds to the classical gravitational field of General Relativity. In this domain, the First Ortho‑Pair (RSR ⟂ RGR) governs the behaviour of massive systems, and the familiar curved gravitational potential appears naturally in r‑space. The transformation w=1/r provides a compactified and linearised view of the same GR potential, making the underlying structure of the mass ortho‑pair easier to visualise.

**Conceptual Role**

The w‑coordinate performs two key functions in the RR framework:

**1. Compactification**

- Points at infinite radius (\(r\rightarrow \infty \)) are brought to a finite coordinate value (\(w=0\).)
- This makes global structures easier to visualise and compare.
- It provides a natural “outer boundary” for diagrams without truncation.

**2. Linearisation of the Potential**

- The classical gravitational potential well in r‑space is curved.
- Under the transformation \(w=1/r,\) the same potential becomes a **straight‑sided cone**.
- This reveals a hidden linear structure that is obscured in r‑space.



**Geometric Expression**

In r‑space:

- The gravitational well is a smooth, curved funnel.
- The radial coordinate increases outward without bound.

In w‑space:

- The well becomes a **linear cone**.
- The apex corresponds to r=0 (i.e., w=1).
- The rim corresponds to \(r=\infty\)  (i.e., w=0).
- The entire geometry fits neatly within a finite domain.

**Interpretive Notes**

- The w‑coordinate is not merely a mathematical convenience; it exposes structural symmetries that are otherwise hidden.
- It provides a natural “compactified” view of radial phenomena, useful for precession, phase, and potential diagrams.
- It integrates cleanly with the RR axis grammar and the Q‑axis vertical structure
