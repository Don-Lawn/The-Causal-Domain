## **M**

### **Mass (Precession Rate)**

**Definition**  

Mass is the rate at which the child’s phase plane precesses around the inherited Q‑axis \(Q_p\).  
It is a geometric tempo, not a substance: a measure of how quickly the intrinsic Q‑axis \(Q_c\) rotates relative to the parent frame.

**Conceptual Role**  

Mass emerges from misalignment.  
When the phase plane is tilted relative to \(Q_p\), the child’s intrinsic axis \(Q_c\) must precess to maintain consistency with the inherited frame.  
The speed of this precession is the mass.

Mass therefore encodes:

- the coupling between \(Q_p\) and \(Q_c\)  
- the dynamical cost of polarisation  
- the temporal twist induced by the phase plane’s orientation  
- the layer’s resistance to changes in alignment  

A perfectly aligned phase plane (no tilt) produces zero precession → zero mass.

**Geometric Expression**  

If \(\theta\) is the polarisation angle between \(Q_p\) and \(Q_c\), then mass is proportional to the precession rate:
$$
m \;\propto\; \frac{d\phi_{\text{prec}}}{dT}
$$
where \(T\) is the parent‑layer time coordinate.

**Cross‑links**  

Polarisation, Precession, \(Q_p\), \(Q_c\), Phase Plane, Alignment, Tempo.

**Interpretive Summary**  

Mass is not an intrinsic property of the layer; it is a geometric consequence of tilt.  
A misaligned phase plane forces the intrinsic Q‑axis to precess around the inherited axis, and the rate of that precession *is* the mass.  
This makes mass a purely geometric, tempo‑driven quantity arising from the relationship between layers in the OP‑stack.



### **Momentum Domain (k‑domain)**

**Definition**  

The energy‑side domain of the OP‑layer, representing the child’s momentum, frequency, and phase‑energy structure.  
It is the Fourier‑dual of the spatial domain (x‑domain).

**Conceptual Role**  

The k‑domain expresses the layer’s **energetic geometry**.  
Where the x‑domain describes spatial distribution, the k‑domain describes:

- momentum distribution  
- frequency content  
- phase structure  
- energetic orientation relative to \(Q_c\)  

The k‑domain is where the k‑sphere lives, and where mass, harmonics, and precession signatures appear.

It is the domain in which the FT reveals the layer’s internal energy architecture.

**Geometric Expression**  

A point in the k‑domain corresponds to a 3‑rotation plane (a full phase‑energy orientation).  
The full domain forms a hypersphere:
$$
S_k^3 = \{\, \mathbf{k} \in \mathbb{R}^4 : |\mathbf{k}| = \text{constant} \,\}
$$
with \(Q_c\) as the canonical vertical axis.

**Cross‑links**  

x‑domain, k‑sphere, Dual Hyperspheres, FT, Phase Rotation, Mass, \(Q_c\).

**Interpretive Summary**  

The momentum domain is the energy‑side expression of the layer’s geometry.  
It is the Fourier‑dual of the spatial domain and reveals the layer’s momentum, frequency, and phase‑energy structure.  
The k‑domain is where the energetic meaning of the layer becomes visible, encoded on the k‑sphere and organised around the intrinsic Q‑axis.
