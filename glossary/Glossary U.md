## U

### **Unit Sphere (x‑sphere / k‑sphere)**

**Definition**  

A sphere of radius 1 used to represent either the spatial distribution (x‑sphere) or the causal energy distribution (k‑sphere) of a layer.

**Conceptual Role**  

The unit sphere provides:

- a normalised geometry for projection  
- a clean separation between shell \((r = 1)\) and centre \((r = 0)\)  
- a common structure for both domains  
- the stage on which ribbon projections appear  

Using a unit radius keeps the geometry scale‑free and emphasises structure over magnitude.

**Geometric Expression**  
$$
\|\vec{x}\| = 1 \quad\text{or}\quad \|\vec{k}\| = 1
$$

**Cross‑links**  

x‑sphere, k‑sphere, Shell–Centre Duality, Radial Coordinate, Projection.

**Interpretive Summary**  

The unit sphere is the normalised stage on which the OP‑layer’s geometry unfolds.  
It provides a clean, scale‑free structure for both spatial and causal distributions.

### **Unaligned Frame**

**Definition**  

A frame whose Q‑axis is not aligned with the observer’s inherited Q‑axis \(Q_p\).  
It is the geometric expression of relative motion or polarisation.

**Conceptual Role**  

An unaligned frame expresses:

- tilt between Q‑axes  
- the need for rotational alignment before projection  
- the geometric origin of relativistic disagreement  
- precession dynamics  

It is the OP‑framework’s analogue of a “moving frame”.

**Geometric Expression**  
$$
Q_c = R(\alpha)\, Q_p
$$

**Cross‑links**  

Tilt, Polarisation, Relativity as Rotation, Alignment, Precession.

**Interpretive Summary**  

An unaligned frame is one whose Q‑axis is rotated relative to the observer’s.  
This misalignment produces the familiar relativistic effects through projection geometry.



### **Unitarity (Rotational Preservation)**

**Definition**  

The property that rotations preserve length, orthogonality, and total energy.  
In the OP‑framework, unitarity ensures that causal rotation does not create or destroy energy.

**Conceptual Role**  

Unitarity guarantees:

- conservation of amplitude in the phase plane  
- preservation of orthogonality between ortho‑pairs  
- consistency of the ribbon’s rotation  
- stability of the dual spheres  

It is the mathematical backbone of the rotation groups SO(2), SO(3), and SO(4).

**Geometric Expression**  
$$
R^\top R = I
$$

**Cross‑links**  

Rotation Group, Phase Rotation, Ortho‑Pairs, Dual Hyperspheres.

**Interpretive Summary**  

Unitarity ensures that rotations preserve structure.  
It keeps the OP‑geometry consistent and guarantees that causal rotation conserves energy.
