Conductive walls are not passive edges.

This immediately produces:

- amplitude suppression near the boundary
- polarisation filtering
- a forced reshaping of the oscillation cycle

The geometry of the wall dictates the geometry of the field.

**2.3 Phase distortion: the oscillation cycle is retimed**

Amplitude cannot be suppressed without affecting phase.

- the line develops a **tilt in its timing**

The phase distortion is therefore not a secondary effect — it is the **direct mathematical consequence** of the wall’s geometry.

$$

Here:

- \mathbf{E_0}(y) is the **local amplitude vector**, already shaped by the boundary.
- \phi (y) is the **local phase**, the oscillation angle of the field at that point.
- Phase is the **projection** of the photon’s internal rotation — the visible shadow of the Ribbon’s deeper twist.

A uniform phase profile corresponds to straight‑ahead propagation.
 A tilted phase profile corresponds to sideways momentum.
 A curved phase profile corresponds to focusing or spreading.

Phase is therefore not an abstract mathematical parameter.
 It is the **geometric state** of the photon across the slit.
