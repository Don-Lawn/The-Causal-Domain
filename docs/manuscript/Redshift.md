# RSR Proof of Equivalence for Redshift 

# Abstract

*This section demonstrates that rotational special relativity (RSR) yields the same redshift relation as Einsteinian special relativity (ESR). By expressing velocity‑based distortion as a rotation into an auxiliary dimension, the RSR framework collapses to the familiar square‑root form of ESR. The proof confirms that the rotational model is mathematically equivalent to the traditional Lorentz treatment of redshift, differing only in interpretation,
 not in result.*

 

# Background

- **Einsteinian Special Relativity (ESR):** Redshift arises from time dilation and length contraction, expressed through the Lorentz factor.

- **Rotational Special     Relativity (RSR):** Redshift is interpreted as a geometric rotation     into the *q* dimension, unifying     inertial and gravitational effects under a single rotational picture.

 

# Introduction

In this proof, we show that the rotational model and the traditional Einsteinian model are mathematically equivalent in their treatment of redshift. The velocity case (RSR) and the gravitational case (RGR) both reduce to the same square‑root structure, differing only in quadrant orientation. This establishes that the rotational framework is not merely an alternative visualization but a valid restatement of relativistic redshift, consistent with established physics.

# ESR baseline

Einsteinian Special Relativity gives the relativistic Doppler redshift as:

$$
\frac{\nu_{\mathrm{obs}}}{\nu_{\mathrm{emit}}}=\sqrt{\frac{1-v/c}{1+v/c}}
$$


 This is the standard Lorentz‑based relation.

# Square‑root structure

Notice that the ESR formula can be rewritten in terms of the Lorentz factor:

$$
\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-\left(v/c\right)^2}}
$$


and the frequency ratio is essentially a square‑root distortion factor tied to velocity.

# RSR rotational form

In Rotational Special Relativity, we define distortion as:

$$
\varphi=cos{\left(\theta\right)}
$$


with the rotation angle   determined by:

$$
{cos}^2{\left(\theta\right)}=1+w 
$$


where w is the normalized velocity parameter (positive in RSR).

# Equivalence

Set \(w=-\left(v/c\right)^2\)   Then:

$$
{cos}^2{\left(\theta\right)}=1-\left(v/c\right)^2
$$

$$
cos{\left(\theta\right)}=\sqrt{1-\left(v/c\right)^2}
$$

But this is exactly the Lorentz factor denominator:
$$
\varphi=cos{\left(\theta\right)}\ =\ 1+w\ 
$$

$$
\Rightarrow\ w=-\left(\frac{v^2}{c^{2)\ }}\right)
$$

$$
\Rightarrow\ cos{\left(\theta\right)}=\sqrt{1-\left(v/c\right)^2}
$$

$$
=\frac{1}{\gamma}
$$



So, the distortion factor in RSR is mathematically identical to the ESR redshift relation.

# Conclusion in RSR form

Thus:

$$
\varphi=cos{\left(\theta\right)}=\sqrt{1+w}
$$


with  \(w=-\left(v/c\right)^2 \)

 This proves that RSR reproduces the ESR redshift relation — the difference is only interpretive: ESR frames it as time dilation, RSR frames it as rotation into the q dimension.
