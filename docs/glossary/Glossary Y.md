## Y

**Y‑Axis (Phase‑Plane y‑axis)**

**Definition**

 The axis in the child’s phase plane orthogonal to the x‑axis.
 Together, the x‑ and y‑axes define the orientation of the phase plane and the measurement of azimuth angle \(\phi\) .

**Conceptual Role**

 The y‑axis provides the second basis direction for the phase plane.
 It is essential for defining \(\phi\), constructing the unit circle, and determining the orientation of the phase plane that generates the intrinsic Q‑axis \(Q_c\).

**Geometric Expression**

**Cross‑links**

 Phase‑Plane Coordinate System, Azimuth Angle \(\phi\), Phase Plane, \(Q_c\), Canonical Axes.

**Interpretive Summary**

 The y‑axis is one of the two basis directions of the phase plane.
 Together with the x‑axis, it defines the geometry of \(\phi\)  and determines the orientation of the plane whose normal becomes the child’s intrinsic Q‑axis.
