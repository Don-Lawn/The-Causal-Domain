

## X

### **x‑Domain (Spatial Domain)**

**Definition**

 The domain representing the child layer’s spatial geometry. It contains the spatial distribution sphere (x‑sphere) and expresses localisation, shape, and spatial extent.

**Conceptual Role**

 The x‑domain is one half of the layer’s dual‑domain structure.
 It expresses the geometry that the Fourier transform maps into the k‑domain.
 Localisation in the x‑domain corresponds to spread in the k‑domain, and vice versa.

**Geometric Expression**
$$
S_x^3=\{ \, \mathbf{x}\in \mathbb{R^{\mathnormal{4}}}:|\mathbf{x}|=\mathrm{constant}\, \} 
$$

**Cross‑links**

Spatial Distribution Sphere, k‑Domain, Dual Hyperspheres, FT, Cross‑Domain Duality.

**Interpretive Summary**

The x‑domain is the spatial face of the layer’s geometry.
 It expresses localisation and shape, and it is transformed into the energy domain by the FT.
 Together with the k‑domain, it forms the dual‑hypersphere architecture of each layer.



### **x‑Sphere (Spatial Distribution Sphere)**

**Definition**

 The 3‑sphere representing the spatial distribution of the child layer.
 It is the Fourier‑dual of the k‑sphere.

**Conceptual Role**

 The x‑sphere encodes how the child’s geometry is distributed in space.
 It is the starting point for the layer‑local FT, which maps this spatial structure into the energy distribution sphere.

**Geometric Expression**


$$
S_x^3=\{ \, \mathbf{x}:|\mathbf{x}|=\mathrm{constant}\, \} 
$$

**Cross‑links**

 Energy Distribution Sphere, Dual Hyperspheres, FT, Canonical Axes.

**Interpretive Summary**

 The x‑sphere is the spatial counterpart to the k‑sphere.
 It expresses the child’s geometry in space and is transformed into the energy domain by the FT, revealing the deep symmetry between spatial and energetic structure.
