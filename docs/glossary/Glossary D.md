### Duality (x ↔ k)

**Definition**
 The fundamental symmetry between the spatial domain (x) and the energy/momentum domain (k), expressed through the Fourier transform.

**Conceptual Role**
 Duality explains why the same geometry appears in both domains and why the FT swaps localisation and spread.
 It is the backbone of the two‑sphere structure.

**Geometric Expression**
 A dual mapping:
$$
x\leftrightarrow k
$$


implemented by the FT.

**Cross‑links**
 FT, Dual Hyperspheres, Cross‑Domain Duality, Canonical Axes.

### **Duality Domains 3D**

The maximum number of dimensions that we can draw on flat surfaces is 3D.  We use this to give a better view of the real situation.

**Definition**

The pair of 3‑spheres associated with each child layer:

- the x‑sphere, representing spatial distribution, and
- the k‑sphere, representing energy/momentum distribution.

They are related by the Fourier transform.

**Conceptual Role**

Dual hyperspheres express the fundamental symmetry between space and energy.
The FT maps the child’s geometry from one sphere to the other, swapping centre and shell.

**Geometric Expression**

Two spheres of equal dimensionality:
$$
S_x^3 \;\longleftrightarrow\; S_k^3
$$
linked by a layer‑local FT.
