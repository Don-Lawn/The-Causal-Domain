## V

### **Vertical Axis (Q‑axis)**

**Definition**  

The axis along which the ribbon advances in Q‑depth.  
It is the layer’s intrinsic orientation and the reference direction for phase rotation and Tempo.

**Conceptual Role**  

The vertical axis expresses:

- the direction of causal progression  
- the inherited orientation from the parent layer  
- the axis around which tilt is measured  
- the backbone of the ribbon’s helical trajectory  
- the distinction between causal and phenomenal domains  

It is the structural spine of the OP‑layer.

**Geometric Expression**  

A unit vector \(Q\) defining the vertical direction in the layer’s causal frame.

**Cross‑links**  

Q‑axis, Tilt, Tempo, Ribbon, Trajectory, Causal Domain.

**Interpretive Summary**  

The vertical axis is the layer’s intrinsic Q‑axis.  
It defines the direction of causal progression and anchors the geometry of rotation, tilt, and time.



### **Vector Pair (Ortho‑Pairs)**

**Definition**  
A pair of orthogonal unit vectors spanning the phase plane.  
They form the basis for representing rotation, phase, and the ribbon’s internal dynamics.

**Conceptual Role**  
Vector pairs provide:

- the 2D basis for phase rotation  
- the decomposition of the ribbon into cosine and sine components  
- the structure used by the Fourier transform  
- the foundation for Ω‑vector construction  

They are the simplest building block of the OP‑framework.

**Geometric Expression**  
$$
\{\hat{e}_1, \hat{e}_2\}, \quad \hat{e}_1 \cdot \hat{e}_2 = 0
$$

**Cross‑links**  

Phase Plane, Ortho‑Pairs, Phase Angle \(\phi\), Rotation–Oscillation Duality.

**Interpretive Summary**  

A vector pair spans the phase plane and provides the orthogonal basis for rotation.  
It is the geometric seed of phase, oscillation, and the ribbon’s internal structure.

### **Velocity (as Tilt)**

**Definition**  

In the OP‑framework, velocity is not a speed but a **tilt** between Q‑axes.  
Relative motion is expressed as angular misalignment, not as a linear quantity.

**Conceptual Role**  

Velocity‑as‑tilt explains:

- relativistic effects as projection geometry  
- disagreement between observers as Q‑axis rotation  
- why Tempo differs between frames  
- why no speed limit needs to be postulated  

Velocity becomes a geometric relation, not a dynamical one.

**Geometric Expression**  
$$
v \;\leftrightarrow\; \alpha = \arccos(Q_p \cdot Q_c)
$$

**Cross‑links**  

Tilt, Q‑axis, Relativity as Rotation, Tempo, Projection.

**Interpretive Summary**  

Velocity is reinterpreted as tilt between Q‑axes.  
Relative motion becomes geometry, and relativistic effects arise from projection rather than postulates.

The oscillatory behaviour that appears when a rotating causal state is projected onto a phenomenal axis.  
Vibration is the observable form of deeper rotational dynamics.

**Conceptual Role**  

Vibration expresses:

- the projection of rotation into the phenomenal domain  
- the emergence of waveforms  
- the link between phase rotation and observable oscillation  
- the basis of signal behaviour  

It is the phenomenal appearance of causal rotation.

**Geometric Expression**  
$$
x(t) = A\cos(\varphi(T))
$$

**Cross‑links**  

Rotation–Oscillation Duality, Projection, Phase Angle, Transform.

**Interpretive Summary**  

Vibration is the oscillatory shadow of causal rotation.  
It is the phenomenal appearance of a deeper rotational structure.
