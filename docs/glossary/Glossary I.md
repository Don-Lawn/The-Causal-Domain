## I

### **Inherited Q‑axis \((Q_p)\)**

**Definition**

The Q‑axis passed down from the parent layer to the child layer.  
It defines the **observer frame** in which the child’s geometry is initially viewed.

**Conceptual Role**

\(Q_p\) is the child’s *starting vertical direction*.  
It determines:

- the observer’s orientation  
- the initial frame in which polarisation is measured  
- the axis that must be rotated during alignment  

\(Q_p\) is not generated by the child; it is inherited.

**Geometric Expression**

A fixed axis supplied by the parent layer:
$$
Q_p = \text{Parent’s intrinsic Q}
$$

**Cross‑links**

\(Q_c\), Polarisation, Alignment, Observer Frame, Axial Inheritance.

**Interpretive Summary**

The inherited Q‑axis is the observer’s vertical direction, passed down from the parent layer and used to measure the child’s orientation.  
It is the extrinsic frame against which the child’s intrinsic geometry is first compared, and the axis that must be aligned to \(Q_c\) before the FT can operate.

### **Intrinsic Geometry (Child Frame)**

**Definition**

The geometry defined relative to the child’s own intrinsic Q‑axis \(Q_c\).  
It is the internal frame in which the child’s phase plane, hyperspheres, and FT structure are naturally expressed.

**Conceptual Role**

Intrinsic geometry is the child’s *true* frame — the one generated by its own phase rotation.  
In this frame:

- the phase plane is horizontal  
- \(Q_c\) is vertical  
- the x‑sphere and k‑sphere take canonical form  
- precession and mass are defined cleanly  

This is the frame the FT requires, and the frame alignment aims to recover.

**Geometric Expression**

A coordinate system based on:
$$
(x_c,\ k_c,\ Q_c)
$$
with \(Q_c\) as the canonical vertical axis.

**Cross‑links**

\(Q_c\), Phase Plane, Canonical Axes, Alignment, FT, Local Axes.

**Interpretive Summary**

Intrinsic geometry is the child’s internal coordinate system, defined by its own Q‑axis and phase dynamics.  
It is the frame in which the layer’s physics is simplest, the FT is valid, and the dual‑hypersphere structure becomes clean and canonical.
