

## B

Boundary Conditions (Parent → Child)

**Definition**

The structural rules that define where one OP‑layer ends and the next begins, specifying what geometric, energetic, and axial information is passed from the parent layer to the child layer.

**Conceptual Role**

Boundary conditions ensure that each layer is self‑consistent while still inheriting essential structure from the layer above. They define the inherited Q‑axis, the orientation frame, the energy constraints, and the limits on how the child may generate its own geometry.

**Geometric Expression**

A mapping
$$
 \mathrm{Parent\  Layer}\rightarrow \mathrm{Child\  Layer}
$$
 that passes down\(Q_p\), Tₚ, orientation, and energy constraints, while requiring the child to generate its own phase plane, \(Q_c\), precession, and hyperspheres.

**Cross‑links**

Axial Inheritance,\(Q_p\), \(Q_c\), Layer Isolation, Parent Layer, Child Layer, FT.

**Interpretive Summary**

 Boundary conditions define the hand‑off between layers: what the child receives from the parent, and what it must generate for itself. They ensure that each layer begins with a consistent inherited frame — the parent’s Q‑axis, orientation, and energy constraints — while still requiring the child to build its own geometry on top of that foundation. By cleanly separating inheritance from self‑generation, boundary conditions prevent cross‑layer mixing and preserve the integrity of the OP‑stack’s recursive structure.
