Overview
The Causal Domain is a physics manuscript exploring the idea that our observable spacetime reality is a Fourier‑like projection of a deeper Wave/Field substrate. In this framework, mass, energy, time, and relativistic structure arise as emergent phenomena generated by transformations between the underlying domain and the phenomenal world we experience.

The project develops a unified geometric interpretation that connects:

Relativity and temporal structure

Energy–Mass duality

Quantum behaviour and uncertainty

Cosmological expansion and redshift

Scale‑invariant patterns from galaxies to quanta

The goal is to articulate a coherent, cross‑scale model in which the “Causal Domain” acts as the generative layer beneath physical reality.

Core Idea
Our normal 3+1D spacetime is treated as a Fourier‑like transform of a deeper Wave/Field domain.
In this view:

Energy corresponds to curvature or rotation in the causal domain

Motivation
Modern physics contains deep conceptual fractures:

Relativity treats spacetime as smooth and geometric

Quantum theory treats reality as discrete and probabilistic

Cosmology struggles with expansion rates, dark energy, and early‑universe structure

Mass–energy equivalence is descriptive but not generative

The Causal Domain framework aims to provide a single underlying mechanism that produces these behaviours as emergent projections of a deeper, continuous, wave‑based substrate.
